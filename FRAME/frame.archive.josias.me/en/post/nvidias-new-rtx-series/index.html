Nvidia's new RTX Series

The promising new gaming era?

The hype as well as the disappointing price point of the new RTX Series of Nvidia has arrived, promising a new generation of gaming.

Nvidia claims for the new Series to have up to six times the performance in ray tracing than the previous generation. But don’t confuse this number with its actual performance and don’t expect your fps to be six times higher than you would get with a current GTX 10 series graphics card.

All the big brands like Asus, MSI and EVGA (the list goes on) are opening RTX 2080 and RTX 2080 Ti up ready for pre-order today, with cards available on September 20th. The price point varies from around $500 to $1,300. And if you just read the for the first time you might be just a tiny bit terrified with that price point. And you are not the only one. Everyone is already debating about that justifiable price.

 

Should I buy?

Sure, real-time ray tracing is a promising new era for gaming and it does have its catch, but is it worth almost double the price than the previous generation? We from Frame Magazine recommend waiting for a while before buying one of the new cards. Maybe even for the 30’s Series. Why? Because prices will most likely sink in the future. And the price will only stay high as long as people are willing to pay for it. It’s understandable from Nvidia’s point of view to set such a high price point. There hasn’t been any grand promising new technology around for a while, so it makes sense to profit from that new generation. But if you are one of the hardcore gamers whose cash is already boiling over your purse don’t discourage yourself from pre-ordering, because nonetheless the price point, the real-time ray tracing technology is a big step for the gaming community.
